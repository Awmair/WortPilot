# WortPilot Content And Curriculum Spec

## Teaching Contract

- No day/week pacing. Use modules and steps.
- Assume learner starts from zero German.
- Define grammar terms plainly on first use.
- Client/business German has priority.
- Every German word/phrase needs audio.
- Accept ASCII fallbacks: `ae`, `oe`, `ue`, `ss`.
- Quizzes must grade explicitly and explain misses.
- Repeated mistakes trigger targeted drills.
- After lessons, ask continue / review / break.

## Known Weak Points

Prioritize these in review and mini-drills:

- `schreiben` vs `sprechen`
- `kaufen`
- `Kunde`
- `heute` spelling
- Comma after email salutations
- Lowercase after salutation except nouns and `Sie/Ihre`
- German `w` sounds like English `v`

## Vocabulary Tracks

Every module should include at least some vocabulary from:

- Email/chat.
- Business.
- Tech.
- AI.
- Sales/agency.
- Grammar/function words.

## Lesson Format

Each lesson should include:

1. Eyebrow: `Modul X · Schritt Y`
2. H1 title.
3. Short lead.
4. Hero German phrase with audio and phonetic.
5. Why this matters.
6. Lettered sections.
7. Tappable examples.
8. Vocabulary banked.
9. Active recall quiz.
10. Recap.
11. Optional go deeper.
12. Continue/review/break action.

## Quiz Question Types

Use mixed formats:

- German to English.
- English to German.
- Fill in blank.
- Multiple choice.
- Spot the error.
- Short production.
- Sound/spelling check.

## Content Quality Rules

- Do not introduce grammar before it is needed.
- Do not assume question words not taught yet.
- Keep business examples realistic.
- Avoid raw machine-translated emails.
- Prefer formal `Sie` with clients.
- Use last names in formal salutations.
- Remind that sign-offs normally do not take a comma.

## Free Resource Policy
